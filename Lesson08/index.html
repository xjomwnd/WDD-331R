Types of Selectors

The universal selector (*) is a simple selector. So are type (or element) selectors such as p and pseudo-element selectors such as ::first-letter. Compound selectors, such as p:last-child or .message.error, are a sequence of simple selectors that reflect a set of simultaneous conditions to meet when applying rules to an element.

    Combinator selectors express a relationship between elements. There are four:
  • the descendant combinator, as in article p
  • the child combinator (>), as in .sidebar > h2
  • the adjacent sibling combinator (+), as in ul + p
  • the general sibling combinator (~), as in p ~ figure
